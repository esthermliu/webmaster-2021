Certified Cats

Animal assisted therapy dates back to 17th century Greece, where horses were used to lift the physical and mental spirits of severely ill people. In wartime America during the second World War, the Red Cross worked with the Army Air Corps to found a farm where recovering soldiers could interact and coexist with animals while recuperating from physical and mental trauma. Historically, animals have been thought to be excellent sources of comfort for humans. What are Therapy Cats?

Therapy cats are any trained felines that aid humans in emotional support through interaction. Cat therapy is often practiced in senior care homes, rehab centers, detention centers, and a number of other areas. They can help prevent a variety of types of suffering, including depression, anxiety, and phobias. Neomedica can refer you to trained, professionally certified, and trustworthy therapy cats. Call 1-800-NEOMEDIC to consult a physician.

How They Help

The benefits of cat therapy are largely linked to the levels of serotonin and oxytocin released when stroking their fur. According to Patricia Pendry, a Washington State University researcher, “one-on-one and group interactions with animals have reduced individuals' cortisol levels in therapeutic and health care settings”. In their study, just ten minutes stroking a cat’s fur led to a significant decrease in stress levels and a notable increase in pleasure.
