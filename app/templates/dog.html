Therapy Dogs

Animal assisted therapy dates back to 17th century Greece, where horses were used to lift the physical and mental spirits of severely ill people. In wartime America during World War II, the Red Cross worked with the Army Air Corps to found a farm where recovering soldiers could interact and coexist with animals while recuperating from physical and mental trauma. Historically, animals have been thought to be excellent sources of comfort for humans. What are Therapy Dogs?

Therapy dogs are any trained canines that aid humans in emotional support through interaction. Dog therapy is often practiced in senior care homes, rehab centers, detention centers, and several other areas. They can help prevent a variety of types of suffering, including depression, anxiety, and phobias. Neomedica can refer you to trained, professionally certified, and trustworthy therapy dogs. Call 1-800-NEOMEDIC to consult a physician.

Gazing into a dog's eyes reduces anxiety

If you are recovering from a mental or physical condition, you may be under a large amount of stress or trauma from it. Therapy dogs help relieve this stress by being providing comfort and relaxation that allows you to escape stress and simply focus on the dog. Research shows that therapy dogs are effective in improving the mental health and well-being of those around them. In addition, research also shows that looking into a dog’s eyes releases antidepressants such as dopamine in the body and allows the patient to feel more compassionate about other people and feel uplifted. This can also bring about a sense of calm that counteracts and eventually replaces stress and trauma.
