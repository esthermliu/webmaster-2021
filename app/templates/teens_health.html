Physical

Although you may be at a prime age, as an adolescent you are still susceptible to injuries and physical issues. Here at Neomedica we equip adolescents with proper knowledge of body mechanics and an estimated recovery timeline. Mental

Your body is changing and so is your mind. Mental instability that comes and goes can be normal and is expected, however, if your anxiety or rage lasts for several months or is seemingly unending, you may need to contact a therapist. Our therapists understand your struggle and will work to repair your relationships with not only others but also with yourself.

Warning: Do not contact or rely on Neomedica if you, a loved one, or a friend are currently contemplating suicide. Instead, immediately contact the National suicide prevention hotline at 1-800-273-8255. The hotline is available 24/7 for you. Once you are free from any immediate danger we can provide personalized, professional help for all issues you.

Sleep

You’re not alone! Research shows that 73% of teens don’t receive the minimum amount of sleep required to function at their best. During puberty, the body’s circadian rhythm (which tells you when to sleep and wake up) resets and may tell you to sleep later than you should be. Insomnia is another major factor that affects quantity and quality of sleep. If you think getting enough rest and you still wake up tired at the start of the day, it may be helpful to contact one of our therapists. They can help you deal with almost all kinds of sleeping challenges.
