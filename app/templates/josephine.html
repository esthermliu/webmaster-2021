About / Leadership / Josephine Greenberg

Overview

Jo is a city lover born in Boston, Massachusetts to two working class parents. Though she struggled to find herself at first, Jo eventually chose to pursue business operations as her career path, and she wouldn’t trade a second of it for the world. After earning her bachelor’s degree in 2006, Jo came in contact with a young woman named Sammy Urtzal, and her life changed forever.

I envision a future where everyone can access world-class telemedicine from wherever they are.

— Jo Greenberg —

Early Life

Little Jo was a simple girl – she liked playing outside, looking at flowers and nature, and reading. None of this changed as she became a pre-teen and eventually an adolescent. She had a knack for painting and drawing, and to this day Jo auctions her artwork at high-profile galleries. One of her most vivid memories from her teenage years was when her father asked her what she wanted to do in life; panicking, she blurted out the first thing that came to mind: “I’ll help run a company!” From that point, she had a prophecy to fulfill.

University Days

Greenberg, thanks to her stellar study skills, was admitted to the University of Pennsylvania. During college, she had two goals – to take care of herself and to focus on her schoolwork. Although balancing these objectives was what she called a “juggling act”, she stayed true to her target and developed excellent self-care methods. She would later apply her principle of finding balance to Neomedica, where she pitched in ideas for their world-class anxiety therapy.

Chasing a Dream

After living comfortably off her salary as an artist for a few years, Jo decided it was time to change things up. Looking for a job at a career fair, she felt hopeless and miserable. She went home, spirits dampened. However, she stumbled across an online job posting advertising Neomedica. She immediately contacted Sammy Urtzal and killed her interview. Today, Greenberg continues to help find balance for Neomedica, even in the strangest, most surreal times.
