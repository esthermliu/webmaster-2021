About / Leadership / Sammy Urtzal

Overview

Sammy Urtzal, Neomedica’s founder and CEO, was born on March 20th, 1987 in Pasadena, California. As the daughter to two Slovakian immigrant parents, Urtzal had a strong passion for the medical field. Although only 34 years of age, Urtzal has founded, developed, and managed the nation’s largest and most reliable telemedicine company.

I envision a future where everyone can access world-class telemedicine from wherever they are.

— Sammy Urtzal —

Early Life

In 1996, aspiring surgeon and excited third-grader, Sammy Urtzal, joyfully sat on her living room floor, playing ‘doctor,’ bandaging her dolls and applying any and every medical spray she could find in her family’s bathroom. She looked up from her toys and marveled at the news channel playing on the boxy 20-inch TV. “Telemedicine: The Future of Health?”. She stared at the illuminated screen in awe, her tiny eight-year-old mind comprehending none of the information but recognizing a place for innovation and revolutionization. Walking to school later that morning, Urtzal tripped on a rock and twisted her ankle, falling onto the pavement. Young Sammy knew that telemedicine was the exact solution she needed.

University Days

Urtzal started attending the University of California, Berkeley at the ripe age of 16 – thanks to her cleverness in high school, she was able to start two years early. Throughout her time in college, she dreamt of pursuing her telemedicine company. While other students partied away, Urtzal was developing early designs of her website. While other students were slacking off or procrastinating, Urtzal stayed ahead of her schoolwork and used her free time to create her business model. Thanks to her proactivity, Urtzal graduated six months early with a degree in psychology and information systems.
