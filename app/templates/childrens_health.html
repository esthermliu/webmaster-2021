Physical

A child’s physical development begins before conception and is highly influenced by parental health and genetic inheritance. Naturally, there is a vital connection between the fetus’s pediatric considerations as well as the mother’s obstetric concerns. (Mothers can check out the Women’s Health section for a more personalized experience) After birth there are many matters to address including, breastfeeding, vaccinations, screening tests and sleeping safety. Our licensed therapists recognize these classic stages of development as well as the need for a flexible treatment suited for your child.

At our appointments, you can expect laid-back care for:

  • Injuries
  • Posture
  • Tone
  • Reflex
  • Balance
  • Strength
  • Range of Motion
  • Weight Control

Emotional

In addition to your child’s physical health, there are also concerns about emotional health. While young, children can be tormented by nightmares and irrational fears. They can have trouble expressing their thoughts and (as you probably know) may easily get sucked up playing video games and watching videos. Here at Neomedica, our therapists are always equipped with statistically proven treatment approaches to get your child feeling comfortable and making friends again.

You can expect treatment for a variety of challenges:

  • ADHD
  • Anorexia
  • Bulimia
  • Autism
  • Asperger Syndrome
  • Learning Disorders
  • Anxiety
  • Depression

Behavioral

Children can and will experience behavioral ups and downs. Life-changing events can take a toll on kids and lead to changes in their behavior. It’s important for parents to recognize the difference between normal changes and behavioral problems. We provide professional care to help not only children but also their parents to aid parents in promoting desirable behavior and reducing unwanted behaviors.

Sports Injuries

Young athletes are more prone to sports injuries than adults. Sports injuries, such as a sprain, bruise, or strain can lead to acute (temporary) pain. In certain cases, your child may develop chronic pain - a pain that lasts for more than three months. If your young athlete complains about pain for longer than three months, it’s important to see an expert in diagnosing and treating chronic pain. Neomedica provides your child with qualified therapists trained in the most modern practices.
